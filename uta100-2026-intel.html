HR-drift test protocol — for ongoing benchmarking

A reviewer asked for a formal aerobic-decoupling test. Here's the protocol, anchored to your race baseline. Decoupling = how much pace-per-heartbeat drops over a steady effort; rising decoupling flags durability or fuelling limits before a race exposes them.

From this race, your aerobic decoupling on flat running, first half vs second half, was ~3.0% (efficiency 0.946 → 0.918). Under 5% on a controlled test is the green zone; your race number is good — consistent with the read that the engine wasn't the limiter.

The test (run it monthly): after a 15-min easy warm-up, run 90 minutes at steady aerobic effort (≈ your power-hike-to-jog transition HR, ~145–150 bpm) on rolling-to-flat terrain, fuelling at your race carb rate. Split it into two 45-min halves and compare pace ÷ HR for each half. Decoupling = (half-1 ratio − half-2 ratio) ÷ half-1 ratio. <5% = aerobic base solid; 5–8% = durability or fuelling gap opening; >8% = address fuelling/heat/endurance before racing. Log it the same way each month — same route, same conditions where possible — so the trend is clean. This is the cheap early-warning system for the exact fade that cost you 100 minutes.

Ground contact time

From the .fit running-dynamics. Longer contact = less elastic, more fatigued legs. Rose 251 → 260 ms — modest, but it tracks the propulsion loss: you spent measurably longer on the ground per step late in the race.

Vertical ratio (running economy)

Vertical oscillation as a % of step length — the cleanest economy metric. Worsened most in P2 (8.5 → 9.3%): in the heat-and-fade window you were bouncing more per metre travelled, wasting energy upward instead of forward.

Whole-body durability

the new #1 · year-round

Progressive long-run volume and weekly back-to-back days (e.g. 3–4 h + 2 h on consecutive days). Heavy lower-body strength 2×/wk: trap-bar deadlift, split squat, calf raises, 4–6 reps @ RPE8. Fuelling rehearsal — 60–90 g carbs/hr from the first hour. This bucket is the gap; treat it as the priority it is.

Priority 2 · ~43 min at stake

Checkpoint choreography

free speed · zero fitness cost

Hard budgets: 2–3 min standard CPs, 8 min night transition, rehearsed twice in training. Drop bags in wearing order. Run-through aid protocol at UTK100 — target ≤18 min total stopped. The single cheapest 40 minutes available to you.

Priority 3 · ~14 min + compounding

Road speed (Sydney)

the Vlad axis · sub-3:20

Sub-3:20 = 4:44/km for 42.2 km. Weekly threshold 4:30–4:50/km, long runs with marathon-pace finishes. Closes the early-pace gap directly and every % compounds across the whole UTA day. The most transferable training you can do.

Priority 4 · ~25 min at stake

Lateral-chain & descent capacity

prevention, not rehab

The descent-specific bucket. Glute-med/min strength: banded side-steps, single-leg RDLs, Copenhagen planks, side-plank w/ abduction — 3×/wk. Then build descent volume (no longer restrict it): weekly downhill repeats to 1,500 m+ single-session by autumn. Re-test: a 2,000 m descent day, pain-free, late in a tired week.

Now → 30 Aug 2026

Marathon block + durability base

Two parallel tracks. Speed: threshold 4:30–4:50/km and long runs finishing at marathon pace. Durability: lower-body strength 2×/wk plus the glute-med/lateral-chain circuit 3×/wk, building the capacity that failed at km 40. Descents are fine to train now — start re-loading them progressively rather than waiting. Fuel every long run at race carb rates to rehearse the gut.

30 Aug 2026

TCS Sydney Marathon — sub-3:20 attempt

Target 4:44/km. Account for the course — the Oxford St climbs and a warm start argue for even effort over even pace, banking nothing early. The result is also a calibration point: a 3:1x validates the road-speed lever the sub-14 model needs is moving; treat anything sub-3:25 on that course as on-track.

Sep → late Nov 2026

Recover, then the UTK build

Two easy weeks post-marathon, then 10–11 weeks converting road speed into mountain shape: descent capacity progressions (build toward 1,500 m+ single-session descent days), hiking strength, heat work, and the checkpoint choreography rehearsed twice — ≤18 min total stopped is the standard.

UTK100 · late Nov/early Dec

Dress rehearsal under race conditions

A-goal 14:30–14:45 stands. Race it as a systems test: HR-capped above 1,800 m, poles-led descent protocol, proactive sodium (heavy-sweater plan). The descent splits and stop total matter more than the finish time.

Dec 2026 → Feb 2027

Durability block

Monster-descent weekends on UTK-proven legs, back-to-back long days, and a weekly threshold touch to hold the marathon speed. This is where the fade band gets trimmed.

Mar → May 2027

UTA100 specific prep

Course simulations: Nellie's at race HR, Kedumba on tired legs at night with poles. Taper with the checkpoint choreography locked.

May 2027

UTA100 — the sub-14 attempt

The model says it's there: 13:51 moving base, marathon-built pace, UTK-proven descents, 16–18 min stops. Then the Miler conversation starts from a real number.

Methodology & caveats (v2). Built from the 1-second Garmin .fit master (61,300 samples incl. power, plus the 60,570-point GPX; an earlier 15-second resample under-counted stopped time and hid the Katoomba auto-pause), official LiveTrail timing (Andre 514th, Vlad Shatrov 13th, and a 10-runner sub-14 cohort), and the 2026 Vert Guide GPX. This version incorporates four independent peer reviews. Key corrections: the original "110 min ITB / 4 min residual" split was re-attributed after running the descent-specificity test on the raw data — past km 40, descents degraded x1.33 but flats (x1.19) and climbs (x1.25) degraded too, so only ~25 min is genuinely descent-specific; ~100 min is whole-body durability; ~14 min is an early-pace gap the original hid. The Phase-1 projection is a band (14:10-14:35 across six fitting windows), not the single "14:03" first quoted. ITB is an inference, not a finding: with one cadence channel and no left/right force data, true gait asymmetry is not computable, and the in-race issue resolved within ~3 weeks with no recurrence (read as a fatigue/heat load-tolerance ceiling, not chronic friction syndrome). Stop detection is threshold-sensitive (43.5 min at <0.8 km/h, 55.3 at <1.2, 67.8 at <1.5) and sits reasonably against the 63-min official mat rest. GPS distance 104.7 vs 101.6 official (~3%); paces GPS-relative. Cohort n=10, 2026 only (LiveTrail bulk endpoints not automatable; 2024 excluded — weather-modified course). Running dynamics (step length, vertical oscillation, performance condition) decoded from the .fit corroborate the power fade; stance-time and L/R balance recorded as no-reading, so true gait asymmetry remains unmeasurable and ITB stays an inference. Power expressed at race weight 67.6 kg from the file. Training prescriptions are written as a running-physio program for discussion with the athlete's own physio, not a clinical diagnosis.
